## The brief

A local MMA club — offering MMA, kickboxing, and BJJ for men, women, and children — needed a
simple site that prospective members could read in either Bosnian or English, check the
weekly schedule, and use to get in touch. The priorities were speed, low cost, and zero
ongoing maintenance.

## What I built

- A **bilingual (BS/EN) site** with a persistent language switcher, so visitors stay in
  their chosen language as they move between pages.
- A **weekly training schedule**, an FAQ, and a **lead-capture contact form** — the core of
  what turns a curious visitor into a trial booking.
- **Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS**, no framework. For a brochure-style site this keeps payloads tiny,
  loads near-instant on mobile, and means there's nothing to keep updated or secure.

## Why it matters

Not every problem needs a framework. Choosing plain HTML/CSS/JS here was a deliberate
trade-off: the fastest possible loads and the lowest possible maintenance burden for a small
business that just needs to be found and contacted.
